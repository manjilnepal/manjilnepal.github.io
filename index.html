Modeling Student Sentiment from Academic Feedback: A Machine Learning Approach

Manjil Nepal, Sunit Soni, M Krishna Siva Prasad, Jayash Shrestha, Prashant Dhimal

16th IEEE ICCCNT - India 2025

abstract | bibtex | code 

Educational quality assessment in academic institutions greatly depends on student feedback which enables both evaluation and development. The analysis of unstructured textual feedback becomes difficult because of its extensive volume and complicated nature. The paper solves these challenges through sentiment analysis which automatically assigns feedback classifications as either positive or neutral or negative. The research adopts Natural Language Processing (NLP) methods to investigate what students think about faculty teaching abilities and course standards. A simulation model replicating real-life situations underwent multiple data processing operations through tokenization followed by stop-words elimination and this was combined with count vectorization to train the models. The evaluation involved implementing three machine learning models which included Naive Bayes, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) alongside Artificial Neural Network (ANN) for sentiment classification. The tested algorithms show their ability to identify feedback sentiments accurately which enables educators and administrators to generate useful insights from the data. Modernized feedback analysis becomes faster and more efficient through this method which delivers quantitative insights for improving both teaching mechanics and curricular structures. The research shows sentiment analysis successfully transforms evaluative expressions into both useful knowledge and actionable insights which support better educational performance and contentment among students.
